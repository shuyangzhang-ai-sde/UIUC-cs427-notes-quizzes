Choosing a style
Plan-driven methods suit mission-, safety-, or financially critical systems that need heavy documentation and control. Prefer agile when requirements change often, the team is small, and rapid feedback matters.
RUP: four phases
RUP Iterative Architecture-centric Use-case Driven
RUP (Rational Unified Process) is an iterative, incremental software process framework: use cases drive requirements, architecture is central to controlling technical risk, and each iteration produces verifiable increments.
PhaseMain goalTypical activitiesKey outputs / milestones
InceptionDecide whether to build and what to buildScope, main features, business case, high-level risksVision, initial requirements, plans
ElaborationProve the architecture can workRefine core use cases; resolve major technical risks; establish and validate the architecture baseline (often including a first runnable build)Architecture baseline, reduced risk, iteration plan
ConstructionComplete the system on that architectureIterative implementation, integration, and testingWorking increments (toward beta / release candidate)
TransitionShip and stabilize in productionFinal testing, deployment, migration, training, defect fixesProduction release / go-live
Exam focus
Inception nails down scope;
Elaboration stabilizes architecture and risk;
Construction delivers features;
Transition covers release and rollout.
Students often mix up Elaboration (architecture and risk) with Construction (full feature delivery on a stable architecture).

  • Claiming tasks 1 and 4 are finish-to-start is the usual wrong option on the quiz.
PERT and the critical path
A PERT chart is a DAG (directed acyclic graph); edge labels are durations. The critical path is the longest path by total duration (not necessarily the most edges).
PathSumTotal duration
A → B → C → E6 + 3 + 716
A → B → D → E6 + 10 + 521 (critical path)
PERT exam checks
  • Edges represent precedence/dependency between activities (not data flow).
  • Edge labels are activity durations/execution times (not importance scores).
  • A valid PERT network is a DAG (directed acyclic graph).
  • Critical-path activities have zero slack; only non-critical activities can have positive slack.
